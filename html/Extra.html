Overview of the fundamentalof information, computer, and network security for secondary schools students

  1. Cyber Security Basics:
    • What is Cyber Security? Cyber security involves protecting computer systems, networks, and data from unauthorized access, attacks, and breaches.
    • Why is it Important? Cyber threats are prevalent, and understanding security mechanisms is crucial to safeguarding personal information, privacy, and digital assets.
  2. Lesson Plans:
    • The Cyber Security Challenge UK offers free lesson plans for secondary school students. These cover various topics related to cyber security and networking. Here are some examples:
      • Computer Misuse Act 1990: Educates pupils about cybercrime, its effects, and introduces the Computer Misuse Act.
      • Staying Safe Online: Helps pupils understand cybercrime and learn how to protect themselves online
      • Ciphers and Cryptography: Introduces encryption methods, ciphers, and problem-solving.
      • Staying on the Right Side of the Law: Educates pupils about cybercrime prevention.
      • Network Security: Covers essential concepts related to securing networks.
      • Wired and Wireless Networks: Explains the differences between wired and wireless networks.
